# Foundation-of-data-analytics1-portfolio
Title of Project: Lucky Food Truck Analysis
Project Description:
This project, completed for a class, involved analyzing data from a Food Truck to help the owner understand factors affecting their business revenue. It consisted of weeks of analysis during which I cleaned data, created charts and pivot tables, and presented findings as actionable insights. In my final analysis, I provide recommendations to improve sales based on season, day of the week, and weather. I created three visually appealing charts that summarize my analysis to improve broader understanding. Throughout this project, I learned to use dashboards more effectively, created pivot tables, and identified outliers. In my future role as a data professional, I know this project will improve the quality of my work because it taught me to perform polished analysis and present actionable insights.

## Process

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Removed missing or duplicate values

### 2. Data Analysis
- Used pivot tables to summarize key trends
- Grouped data by categories (e.g., region, type, time period)

### 3. Visualization
- Created charts (bar charts, line graphs, etc.)
- Identified patterns and comparisons

### 4. Insights & Recommendations
- Found key trends in the dataset
- Developed recommendations based on findings
  

## Key Findings
- Insight & Recommendation 1: Summer and Fall drive about 73% of sales, while Spring sees a drop to 26.97%. Lucky should focus on strategic marketing in the Summer and Fall. For the Spring, Lucky should consider finding different locations to host his truck to reduce the seasonal loss in profits.
- Insight & Recommendation 2: Revenue reaches its highest on Saturday ($33,806) and Sunday ($30,685), while Thursday is the slowest day of the week, bringing in $19,154. Lucky should consider having his fastest and most experienced team members scheduled to work on Saturday and Sunday to maximize profits.
- Insight & Recommendation 3: Sales on Sunny days ($134,054) are over 8 times higher than on Rainy days ($15,917). On days with a 70% chance of rain, Lucky should consider switching to a different menu of items that make more profit, don't spoil quickly, and are cheap and easy to prepare. This could help reduce loss on Rainy days.
